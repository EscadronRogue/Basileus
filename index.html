Basileus Round 0 Setup

BASILEUS

A game of dynastic profiteering inside the Byzantine Empire

How to PlayRules

Goal

Be the richest dynasty when the invasion deck runs out. Gold on hand plus your next round of income decides the winner. If invaders sack Constantinople, the empire falls and the game ends immediately.

A Round, Step By Step

  1. Invasion drawn. A new threat appears with a route through the provinces.
  2. Administration. Provinces pay out gold and raise levies automatically.
  3. Court. Buy estates, appoint officers, gift land to the church, recruit troops, hire mercenaries. Confirm when done.
  4. Secret orders. Each player privately sends every troop to either the Capital (vote in the coup) or the Frontier (fight the invasion), and picks who to back for the throne.
  5. Resolution. Coup is decided first, then the war. Lost provinces become occupied; reconquerable ones return as free-citizen land only when defenders restore them.
  6. Cleanup. Pay 1 gold per professional troop in upkeep. Levies and mercenaries disband.

Provinces & Money

Each province has four numbers: P (profit to its private owner), T (tax to the empire), L (levies it raises), C (gold to the church pool, most provinces are 0).

  • Buying land costs 2×P. The owner collects P every Administration. Privately owned land also raises one fewer levy.
  • Tax cascade on regional tax: the first coin goes to the Basileus, the second to the regional Domestic or Admiral, then the cycle repeats. The Church receives no share of provincial tax. Constantinople tax cycles Basileus → Empress → Chief of Eunuchs.
  • Strategos shortcut. A province with an appointed Strategos sends all its tax and levies straight to that Strategos, bypassing the cascade.
  • Church pool. Each province with C > 0 sends C gold every round to the church pool. The Patriarch takes 2 shares first, then each Bishop in seniority order takes 1 share, cycling until the pool is empty.
  • Church gift. Donating your land to the church sets its profit and tax to 0; its C value becomes the old P+T. You automatically become its Bishop. Bishops can only be appointed in provinces with C ≥ 1; lost provinces keep their bishop but stop contributing to the pool.

Titles & Appointments

  • Basileus (the emperor) may appoint minor titles: Empress, Chief of Eunuchs, a Strategos, or a Bishop.
  • Domestic of the East / West and the Admiral may appoint Strategoi in their region.
  • Patriarch may appoint Bishops, paid with the doubled gold appointment cost.
  • After appointing yourself, appointing someone else unlocks self-appointment again.

Armies

  • Levies appear during Administration and disband at end of round. They are tied to the office that raised them.
  • Professional troops are permanent: at most 1 recruit per office per round, and each costs 1 gold of upkeep every Cleanup.
  • Mercenaries are hired in Court and disband at end of round. The cost rises triangularly: 1, then +2, then +3, etc. The whole company deploys to one place.
  • Capital-locked offices (Empress, Patriarch, Chief of Eunuchs) cannot send their troops to the frontier — they always defend the throne.

Coup & War

  • Coup. Add up the Capital troops backing each candidate. Most votes wins. Ties favour the sitting Basileus. A new Basileus redistributes all four major titles among the other players.
  • War. Frontier troops minus invader strength. Win → reconquer occupied provinces along the route (cost 1, then +1, +1...). Lose → invader advances along the route capturing provinces (same triangular cost). If the invader reaches Constantinople, the empire falls.

Imperial Revocation

Major titles move only during the post-coup purge. During Court, the Basileus can revoke minor titles or estates. The Patriarch can revoke Bishops. The Domestic of the East/West and the Admiral can revoke a Strategos in their region. A title appointed this turn cannot be revoked until next turn. Most revocations in the same round (per player) cost more troops than the last (1, 2, 3, ...), spent from any office or army the player controls. Levies are spent first; professionals are sent on the mission — they still pay upkeep this round and return next round. Patriarch Bishop revocations use doubled per-revocation gold costs: 2, 4, 6... Bishop appointments still use doubled repeat-target gold costs: 0, 2, 4...

Debt & Defenders

  • Debt. A player who enters a turn in debt automatically disbands 1 random professional troop per gold owed. Players in debt also cannot recruit new professional troops until they balance their books.
  • Best defender. When the empire can reconquer provinces, the top frontier contributors take turns choosing either to restore a province to the empire as free-citizen land or take 2×P gold. Gold leaves the farthest pending province along the invasion route occupied, keeping the frontier contiguous.
